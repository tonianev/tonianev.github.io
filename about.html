About

Analytics with a practical, systems-first mindset

I’m Toni, a data analyst focused on turning noisy information into decisions teams can actually use. I care about clarity, repeatability, and outputs that hold up in real-world workflows.

My work typically sits at the intersection of analytics, process understanding, and communication: defining useful metrics, structuring reporting, and helping stakeholders act on what the data says (and what it does not say).

Analytics Reporting Systems Operations Experiment Analysis Stakeholder Communication

Short version

I like building analytics tools and reporting systems that reduce ambiguity, speed up decisions, and make teams less dependent on ad hoc requests.

Working style

Principles I use when building analytics work

  • Decision-first framing: define the action a report or analysis is supposed to support before building it.
  • Shared definitions: keep KPI logic and business terms consistent so teams can trust comparisons.
  • Usability over novelty: the best dashboard is the one people can use in five minutes under pressure.
  • Context matters: numbers are more valuable when paired with constraints, caveats, and likely drivers.

Where I add value

When a team has reporting that exists but is hard to trust, hard to maintain, or hard to use for decisions.

What I enjoy most

Finding the “decision bottleneck” behind a data request and designing a repeatable fix instead of a one-time answer.
